Keep The Ledger Immutable

The Immutable Ledger is independently published. It has no investors. It has no editorial board. It has no parent company conducting a strategic review of its portfolio. The only thing standing between you and another 112-page consultation paper going unread is this publication and your willingness to contribute to it.

We cover payments, fintech, regulation, AI, and the people who gather in large convention centres to discuss all of the above without, in several documented cases, reaching a conclusion.

We read the full reports. We attend the conferences. We count how many times "collaboration" is mentioned during the morning programme. We do this because someone has to, and because the industry has not yet automated it, though a working group has been established to examine the feasibility of doing so.

Your support — at any level — keeps the site running, the ticker animated, and the team resourced. By "team" we mean the people who write the articles, fact-check the satire, and occasionally have to explain to a legal contact that no, we did not actually name anyone specifically.

What Your Support Covers

Hosting and infrastructure. The Immutable Ledger runs on servers. The servers require payment. The payment is processed via a major international card scheme. The irony has been noted. It appears in Appendix C of our internal budget review. Appendix C has not been actioned.

Conference attendance. Accurate reporting on industry events requires being at those events. Industry events charge between $995 and $4,995 per ticket. We attend so you can read about the eighteen times a senior executive said "pivotal moment" without having to be there in person.

Report reading. Industry consultancies and regulators publish extensive documents on a regular basis. These documents contain findings, recommendations, and appendices. We read the appendices. This is a service.

The ticker. The breaking news ticker at the top of the site requires ongoing curation. The industry provides the content. We count it, condense it, and ensure it scrolls at the correct speed. The correct speed is 60 seconds per cycle. This was determined via a consultation. The consultation produced a recommendation. The recommendation was implemented.
